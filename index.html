About Lovelace

Augusta Ada King-Noel, Countess of Lovelace was an English mathematician and writer, chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. Her notes on the engine include what is recognised as the first algorithm intended to be carried out by a machine. As a result, she is often regarded as the first computer programmer.

Brief Timeline

  • Dec 1815 - Born under the name Ada Augusta Byron , she was the only child of the poet George Gordon the 6th (Lord Byron) and his wife, Anne Isabella "Annabella" Milbanke (Baroness Wentworth).
  • Jan 1816 - Parents Separate
  • Apr 1824 - Lord Byron (Ada's father) dies in a war in greece when she was eight years old.
  • Jan 1828 - Ada designs a flying machine.
  • Oct 1829 - Ada gets measles and is unable to leave bed. This last 3 years.
  • Apr 1832 - Recovers from measles. She can walk with crutches.
  • Jan 1833 - Ada meets Charles Babbage. He disscusses he's difference engine with her. She is inspire by his words.
  • Jul 1835 - Ada weds William King.
  • May 1836 - Ada's first child is born.
  • Sep 1837 - Ada's second child is born
  • Jun 1838 - Ada becomes Countess of Lovelace
  • Jul 1839 - Ada's third child is born
  • Aug 1843 - Ada's translation and Notes on the analytical engine are published. This is her life's achievement; inside is what is consider to by the worlds first computer program.
  • Nov 1852 - Lovelace died at the age of thirty-six from uterine cancer and bloodletting by her physicians. She was buried, at her request, next to her father at the Church of St. Mary Magdalene in Hucknall, Nottingham.
